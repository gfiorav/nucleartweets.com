About

This online Geiger counter is an artistic side project by Guido Fioravantti @engineerGuido and Carlos Sánchez @chocotuits. The purpose of the site is to imagine what a "Global Geiger Counter" would look like, and how it could detect potential nuclear incidents before they are reported by traditional media sources. We believe that in the event of a nuclear incident, local residents will spread the news on Twitter first.

To achieve this, the site constantly scans Twitter for tweets containing the term "nuclear". If you see a low radiation reading, it could be related to discussions about nuclear energy or plants, which is considered background radiation. However, if a real nuclear incident occurs, we expect the radiation reading to be extremely high.

Please note that there is a possibility for false positives, so don't panic if you see a high reading. We will only know the true effectiveness of our Geiger counter in the event of a real nuclear incident.

The idea for this site came to us while watching the 1959 film On The Beach. The movie depicts a group of Australians waiting for the end of the world after a global nuclear war. You may come across some references to the movie while using the site.
